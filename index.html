📘 Organic Chemistry

Question 1
What is the number of isomers having molecular formula C4H10 ?
A) 1
B) 2
C) 3
D) 4
✅ Correct Answer: B) 2
C4H10 is butane. It has two structural isomers: normal butane and isobutane.

📖 Explanation

Isomers are compounds that have the same molecular formula but different structural formulas. Butane (C4H10) has:

1. n-butane → straight chain
2. isobutane → branched chain

Therefore, the total number of isomers is 2.

Question 2
According to Wöhler theory, organic compounds can be prepared in:
A) Living cells only
B) Laboratories and living cells
C) Houses only
D) Plants only
✅ Correct Answer: B) Laboratories and living cells
Wöhler prepared urea in the laboratory proving that organic compounds can be synthesized artificially.

📖 Explanation

Friedrich Wöhler synthesized urea from ammonium cyanate. This destroyed the old theory stating that organic compounds can only be produced inside living organisms.

Question 3
Which type of bond joins atoms in hydrocarbon molecules?
A) Ionic
B) Covalent
C) Metallic
D) Hydrogen
✅ Correct Answer: B) Covalent
Hydrocarbons contain covalent bonds between carbon and hydrogen atoms.

📖 Explanation

Carbon atoms share electrons with hydrogen atoms. Shared electron pairs form covalent bonds. Therefore hydrocarbons are covalent compounds.

Question 4
Heating potassium cyanate with ammonium chloride produces:
A) Methane amide
B) Ethylamine
C) Urea
D) Acetamide
✅ Correct Answer: C) Urea
The reaction forms ammonium cyanate first which rearranges into urea.

📖 Explanation

Potassium cyanate reacts with ammonium chloride forming ammonium cyanate.

NH4OCN → CO(NH2)2

This is the famous Wöhler synthesis reaction.
